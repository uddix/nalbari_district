Mahmara Pukhuri Chandkuchi

According to history, this pond/tank has been built by one of the members of the Ahom Dynasty. This pond was built after sacrificing a buffalo at the spot and is thus considered sacred owing to some age old prejudices. The sacrifice rites and rituals were performed to achieve upliftment from the curse of water scarcity in the region. Hence, the pond received the name “Mahmara Pukhuri”.

Popular Things to do & See around

Apart from enjoying the scenic beauty of this place with an evening or noon stroll, you can enjoy a picnic with your friends and family all along the premises of Mahmara Pukhuri. It is also a spot adored by bird watchers and nature lovers.

Getting There

To reach Mahmara Pukhuri, you can take direct rental cab from Lokpriya Gopinath Bordoloi International Airport (GAU) and the drive takes maximum of 1 and half hours. You can avail taxis from any part of Nalbari to reach Mahmara Pukhuri. Even from Paltan Bazaar Railway Station that is connected to Guwahati, Kolkata and Delhi you can take taxis and reach Mahmara Pukhuri within an hour. From Guwahati, you can avail for several state-run and private buses that take you straight to Mahmara Pukhuri in Nalbari.
